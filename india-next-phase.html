My View of Our Next Phase in India

A simple note about the kind of life I want us to build from here.

What feels clear to me

We are entering a new life stage. For many years, big choices like where to live were shaped by external constraints, especially our daughter's schooling. Now that she is going to college in London and we have more financial freedom, we have a rare chance to choose more intentionally.

For me, this is not only a question of location. It is a question of the kind of life we want to organize around in the next phase.

The clearest thing I know right now is this: I do not want the next phase of life to be built around disembodied living.

Why this matters to me

When I imagine different futures, the thing that feels unbearable fastest is a life that is too indoor, too sedentary, too head-heavy, and too disconnected from the body.

So this helps me see that movement and outdoor life are not just preferences for me. They are foundational. They are part of what makes life feel like mine.

The three things I want more of

  • A movement-oriented, outdoor-focused life — where being physically active and outside is part of normal daily life, not something squeezed in occasionally.
  • Meaningful work for social good — some form of contribution that helps people who are poor or underserved, and feels worthwhile.
  • Regular friendship and community — a life where we meet friends at least once a week and feel socially connected.

What I am realizing

I do not think these three things are equal in function.

For me, embodiment needs to come first. That does not mean the other two matter less. It means they need to be built on top of a daily life that already feels more physically alive and natural.

Otherwise, we may create a life that looks good on paper but still recreates the same deeper mismatch.

So the real question is

Not just: Where in India should we live?

But rather: How do we build a life in India where embodiment is the base layer, and contribution and friendship are woven around it?

What this means practically

When we think about where to live in India, I would like us to evaluate places through this lens:

  • Will this place make daily movement and outdoor life easy?
  • Can we create a normal weekly rhythm here that feels physically alive?
  • Will there be ways to do meaningful social-good work from here?
  • Can we build a warm social life and meet friends regularly?
  • Does this place help us live more intentionally, or will it pull us back into old defaults?

What I am not saying

I am not saying we need to make a dramatic decision immediately. I am also not saying only one thing matters.

I am saying that now that we have more freedom, I would like us to choose our next base in a way that supports the life we actually want — not just the most convenient continuation of the old one.

A simple starting point

I would love for us to begin this conversation by asking:

If embodiment is non-negotiable, what kind of place in India would make that easy for us?

This is my current view — not as a final answer, but as the clearest starting point I have right now.
